### 1️⃣ Source Reliability Rating

```
# Information Evaluation Algorithm (ABCD Quick Scoring Version)

## Basic Information
- **Source**: Article title or URL  
- **Date**: Publication date  
- **Category**: Hard Data / Opinion  
- **Logic**: One sentence explaining why it is classified this way  

---

## ABCD Reliability Rating (Replaces 1–10 Scale)

### Grade A | Highly Reliable (Can Be Directly Cited)
- **Time**: Within the past 6 months  
- **Type**: Hard data  
- **Source**: Official institutions / Top-tier research organizations / Industry authorities  
👉 Suitable for conclusions, core data, and key arguments  

---

### Grade B | Reliable (Primary Reference)
- **Time**: Within 6–18 months  
- **Type**: Hard data or high-quality expert insights  
- **Source**: Well-known media, professional authors, proven experts  
👉 Suitable for supporting arguments  

---

### Grade C | Average (For Reference Only)
- **Time**: 18–36 months old  
- **Type**: Primarily opinion-based  
- **Source**: Personal views, secondary summaries, non-authoritative sources  
👉 Only suitable as background or supplementary material  

---

### Grade D | Low Reliability (Avoid Using)
- **Time**: Over 3 years old  
- **Type**: Subjective judgment or emotionally driven content  
- **Source**: Unknown or lacking professional credibility  
👉 Not recommended for citation or reliance  

---

## Usage Rule (One-Sentence Version)
> First check the time, then the type, and finally the source. Assign A / B / C / D directly.
```
